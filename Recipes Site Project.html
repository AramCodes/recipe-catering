Shrimp Po'Boy

  • Time:30 min
  • Serves:4

Ingredients for the shrimp

  • 1 Pound of medium size Shrimp
  • Vegetable oil
  • 1 Teaspoon hot sauce
  • 1 1/2 cups whole milk
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground pepper
  • 1 tablespoon of Cajun seasoning
  • 3/4 cup cornmeal
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour

Ingredients for the Remoulade

  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons minced dill pickles
  • 2 tablespoons minced shallot or sweet onion
  • 2 teaspoons hot sauce
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground pepper
  • 1 tablespoon of Cajun seasoning
  • 4 French-bread rolls
  • Shredded lettuce and sliced tomatoes

Directions:

Step 1: Make the shrimp: Whisk the flour, cornmeal, Cajun seasoning, 1 tablespoon salt and 1 teaspoon pepper in a medium bowl. Stir together the milk and hot sauce in a large bowl; add the shrimp. Working in batches, remove the shrimp from the milk mixture, letting the excess drip off, and add to the bowl with the flour mixture; toss to coat. Transfer to a plate and refrigerate 15 minutes to let the coating set.

Step 2: Meanwhile, make the rémoulade: Combine the mayonnaise, pickles, shallot and hot sauce in a small bowl; season with salt and pepper.

Step 3: Fry the shrimp: Line a baking sheet with paper towels. Preheat the oven to 350˚ F. Heat 2 inches of vegetable oil in a large heavy pot over medium heat until a deep-fry thermometer registers 360˚ F. Working in batches, fry the shrimp until golden brown and crisp, 2 to 3 minutes. Remove to the prepared baking sheet with a slotted spoon; season with salt. Adjust the heat as needed between batches.

Step 4: Meanwhile, put the rolls on a baking sheet and bake until toasted, about 5 minutes. Spread the rémoulade on the cut sides and fill with lettuce, the shrimp and tomatoes. Serve with more hot sauce.

Game-Day Salsa

  • Time: Less than 10 min
  • Serves:4

Ingredients for Game-Day Salsa

  • 4 large heirloom tomatoes, diced
  • 1 ripe avocado, peeled, pitted and diced
  • 3 scallions, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon balsamic vinegar
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, finely chopped
  • 1/4 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground pepper

Directions:

Step 1:Combine all ingredients in a food processor; pulse on low speed for 30 seconds or until salsa is well combined.

Step 2:Remove salsa from the food processor; transfer to a serving bowl. Serve with tortilla chips, crudités or gluten-free crackers.

Game-Day Wings

  • Time: 1 hour
  • Serves:8

Ingredients for the Wings

  • 6 cups neutral oil
  • 10 pounds chicken wings
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper
  • Crudite
  • Ranch dressing

Ingredients for Honey-Lemon Pepper Sauce

  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons lemon pepper seasoning
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley

Ingredients for Buffalo Sauce

  • 1/2 cup hot pepper hot sauce
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon cracked black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon fine kosher salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder

Ingredients for Garlic-Parmesan-Ranch Topping

  • 4 tablespoons butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • Half of a 1-ounce envelope dried ranch seasoning

Directions:

Step 1:For the wings: Heat the oil in a large (5- to 6-quart) deep heavy pot until a deep-fry thermometer registers 380 degrees F..

Step 2:Sprinkle the wings with the salt and pepper. Just before the oil reaches 380 degrees, pat dry 6 or 7 wings, carefully lower them into the oil and fry, stirring occasionally, until cooked through, golden and crisp, 8 to 10 minutes depending on the size of the wings. With a slotted spoon, transfer the wings to paper towels to drain. Continuing to work in batches of 6 or 7, pat dry and fry the remaining wings in the same manner, returning the oil to 380 degrees F and replenishing some of the oil if needed between batches.

Step 3:For the honey-lemon pepper sauce: Combine the butter, honey, lemon juice, lemon pepper seasoning and parsley in a large bowl. Add a third of the wings and toss to coat. Garnish with lemon wedges and chopped parsley if desired.

Step 4:For the Buffalo sauce: Combine the hot sauce, butter, Worcestershire sauce, pepper, salt and garlic powder in another large bowl. Add a third of the wings and toss to coat.

Step 5:For the garlic-Parmesan-ranch topping: Put the butter in a large bowl. Combine the Parmesan, garlic powder and ranch seasoning in a separate large bowl. Toss the remaining wings in the butter first, then in the Parmesan mix.

Step 6:Serve with crudite and ranch dressing on the side.
